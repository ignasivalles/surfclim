# About surfclim

## What is this?

surfclim is a citizen-science project that monitors seawater temperature along the Cantabrian coast — one data point at a time, sampled while surfing.

## Why does it matter?

The ocean is warming. But coastal seawater temperature is surprisingly hard to measure: satellites struggle near the coast, and research vessels don't surf. Surfers do.

Every time someone paddles out with an EnvLogger sensor attached to their board, they contribute a real measurement to the record.

## How it works

A small temperature sensor (EnvLogger) is attached to the surfboard leash. It logs water temperature throughout the session. After the surf, the data is sent automatically and processed to extract the actual water temperature — filtering out air exposure and using a minimum-variance algorithm to identify the true immersion period.

## Reference climatology

Observations are compared against a historical baseline: daily temperature records from Sardinero beach, Santander, spanning 1970–1978. This allows us to compute anomalies and track how today's ocean temperature compares to the past.

## Who is behind it?

This project is part of [Poniendo Ojos al Mar](https://pom-cost.github.io), a citizen-science initiative based in Santander, Spain.
